A family firm, built to go anywhere.

Pansolve began the way it operates: with two people who take problems personally — and who believe the human element is the whole point, not the part to automate away.

We’re Anu and Vikram — a wife-and-husband team, and the founders of Pansolve. We started our working lives in the New York–New Jersey corridor, learning the kind of rigor that busy, high-stakes industries demand. Along the way we fell for California, and we’ve carried both coasts’ habits with us ever since: East Coast discipline, West Coast optimism.

We built Pansolve because we kept seeing the same thing happen. Companies were promised that software and AI would make the hard, detailed work disappear — the medical coding, the reconciliations, the data quality. Then the denials piled up, the books didn’t tie out, and the models shipped confident, wrong answers. The work hadn’t disappeared. It had just lost its humans.

The name says what we believe. Pan is Greek for “all” — all problems, solved by people. We’re the human layer: trained specialists who verify, correct, and improve the work that machines start.

As we grow into a global firm, that stays the founding rule. A family-run standard of care, delivered at whatever scale the work demands — whether that’s one careful reviewer or a dedicated team of fifty.
