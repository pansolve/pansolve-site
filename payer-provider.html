01 / Payment Integrity

Pay what's owed. Not more, not less, not twice.

What it is

Payment integrity ensures a claim was billed correctly and paid according to policy rules — before or after the money moves.

Why it matters

Payers process millions of claims, and errors are routine: duplicate billing, wrong procedure codes, upcoded services, unbundled procedures billed separately, and non-covered services paid by mistake. Each one is money leaking from the system.

What our team does

  • Verifies the claim was billed correctly against coding standards
  • Checks the payment amount against policy and contract rules
  • Flags duplicates, unbundling, upcoding, and non-covered services
  • Identifies patterns that suggest fraud, waste, or abuse
Pre-pay reviewPost-pay recoveryFWA detectionContract compliance

02 / Risk Adjustment

Sicker patients cost more. Prove it, properly.

What it is

Risk adjustment is how insurers — especially Medicare Advantage plans — get paid accurately for how sick their members actually are. Documented chronic conditions translate into risk scores, and risk scores translate into payment.

Why it matters

If a condition isn't documented and coded, the plan is underpaid for that member's real cost of care. If it's coded without support, the plan is exposed in an audit. Accuracy has to cut both ways.

What our team does

  • Reviews medical records for chronic conditions — diabetes, hypertension, COPD, kidney disease, heart failure, and more
  • Confirms each condition is properly documented and supported by physician notes
  • Assigns the correct ICD-10 codes mapped to the right HCC categories
  • Flags unsupported codes before they become audit findings
Medicare AdvantageICD-10 / HCCRetrospective reviewRADV readiness
Worked example

Physician's note reads:

"Patient has Type 2 Diabetes with complications."

But the submitted claim missed the diagnosis entirely — the member's risk score doesn't reflect their real condition.

Coder adds the supported ICD-10 code. Payment now matches the patient. ✓

03 / Chart Review

The claim says one thing. What does the chart say?

What it is

Chart review is the careful reading of a patient's full medical record — doctor notes, lab reports, diagnoses, procedures, and medications — to verify that what was claimed is what actually happened.

Why it matters

Documentation is the evidence behind every claim. When the chart and the claim disagree, someone is being paid for care that isn't supported — or a patient's record is missing something that matters.

What our team does

  • Validates diagnosis and procedure accuracy against the full record
  • Confirms medical necessity for services billed
  • Identifies missing or contradictory documentation
  • Ensures compliance with payer and regulatory rules
Clinical validationMedical necessityDocumentation gapsCompliance
Worked example

The claim states:

Patient had pneumonia  suspected, not confirmed

The chart shows only suspected pneumonia — no confirming imaging or lab result in the record.

Inconsistency flagged before it becomes a compliance problem. ✓

04 / Coding Audits

Who checks the coders? We do.

What it is

A coding audit is a structured quality check on coding itself — whether done by your in-house team, a vendor, or an AI tool. Even trained coders make mistakes: wrong ICD-10 codes, missed secondary diagnoses, incorrect CPT codes, upcoding and undercoding.

Why it matters

Coding errors compound. A pattern of small mistakes becomes denied revenue, overpayment liability, or an external audit finding. Measuring accuracy is the only way to improve it.

What our team does

  • Independently recodes a statistically meaningful sample of claims
  • Compares results against the original coding and measures the error rate
  • Identifies patterns — by coder, code family, or service line
  • Delivers targeted coder training and process fixes to prevent repeats
Sample recodingError-rate reportingCoder educationAI-output audits
